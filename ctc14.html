Project Details

Our Aim

In 2006-2007 an excavation took place of St Nicholas Kirk, Aberdeen. It uncovered over a thousand sets of human remains, a rich collection of artefacts, and structural parts of the building going back over a thousand years. Much of the post-excavation analysis and research is still ongoing. Using photographs, paper records, some physical artefacts, and a 3D point-cloud data of a scan of the interior of the church, the aim of this weekend is to collate data and make it available to the public.

Interactive Prototypes

Create digital artefacts, and create prototypes to interact with, and visualise with the finds

Visualisations

Experiment with technologies such as 3D visualisation of remains, recovered articles and the building itself

Public Understanding

Assist public understanding, deliver educational benefits and increase awareness of the archaeology and the Mither Kirk project
